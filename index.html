Our Mission

Executive Summary

The Wildfire Project is a non-profit organization that seeks to address the always-relevant issue of wildfires. Wildfires are destructive. They take homes, ecosystems, and lives. We believe we must track current wildfires, educate the misinformed, raise awareness for our cause, create a safe space for victims, and refer the eagerness for opportunities to help.



Mission Statement

At The Wildfire Project, we pledge ourselves to the care of wildfire victims and the prevention of possible wildfires. We aim to make positive change, one step at a time, to protect ourselves, our homes, our families, our livelihoods, and our planet. Every action we take today is to create a safer, more sustainable environment for tomorrow. In every wildfire, every second counts. Join us today.



How to Avoid Wildfires

  • Avoid outdoor activities involving fire or sparks on hot, dry, and windy days.
  • Carry essential firefighting tools like a shovel, bucket, and fire extinguisher in your vehicle.
  • Ensure cigarettes are properly extinguished and never discard them in dry vegetation.
  • Keep campfires small, contained, and away from flammable materials.
  • Always supervise campfires and make sure they are completely extinguished before leaving.
  • Maintain vehicles to prevent sparks from exhaust systems and dragging metal parts.


How Wildfires Start

Most wildfires are caused by human activities:

  • Equipment malfunction, such as power lines or machinery sparks.
  • Discarded cigarettes that ignite dry vegetation.
  • Unattended campfires that spread due to wind.
  • Arson and intentional fire-setting.
